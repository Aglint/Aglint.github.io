Feel the Pulse of Higher Education

发表于2021-07-27|更新于2021-07-28
|阅读量:

 Welcome to my blog.This is my first blog post,but I don’t know what to write in it.So I just write down my paper which was honored as Honorable Mention in 2021 MCM/ICM.

summary

 Higher education is an important stage of education for a country to cultivate talents who can serve the society. However, so far, there is no unified model to scientifically and effectively measure whether a country’s higher education system is healthy and sustainable. This paper constructs an effective model to evaluate the higher education system of any country and formulates new policies for low-evaluation countries.Meanwhile, we reasonably predict the future development prospects of higher education.

 In Task 1,we established a higher education investment model and Talent Cultivation Quality Evaluation Model based on the most common influencing factors. And in order to eliminate the gap between developed and developing regions, we constructed the Service Quality Evaluation model.After that, the factor analysis method is used to clarify the influence of the three sub-models to the final comprehensive evaluation score. According to the model, the United States, China, and Japan were evaluated. The results show that China is the worst among them and its score is only 33.7% of the United States and Japan. It proves that it has a lot of room for improvement. Therefore, we choose China as our further research object.

 In Task 2,according to the real situation of China, we propose some beautiful visions for it from three aspects: Economic inputEducational process and Teaching system. For the purpose of achieving them, we have formulated a three-step strategy to increase the enrollment rate of higher education, and require the government to provide more than 1.5% of GDP for higher education through funding, fundraising and other methods. In addition, the status and salary of educators should also an increase of more than 5% .

 In Task 3,we use our model to measure and compare the health of both the current system and the proposed. It indicated that the comprehensive evaluation score of the Chinese model increased by about 187%, especially the contribution score of the talent evaluation model reached 2.32 times the original. Then we utilize the CIPP evaluation method. By classifying the four evaluation directions into three levels of indicators, we have more strictly proved the scientific rationality and operability of our policies.

 In Task 4,discussing the real-world impacts of implementing your plan means we need to further analyse the effect of the policies and forecast the condition in the future. For education system, we forecast the demographic changes at different levels of education in China with the help of the ISSA model. the conclusion is that after the implementation of the new adjustment, the number of people in higher education is 1.2 times the original natural change, and the number of people without education is reduced to about 18%.For society and the country, our policy has stimulated progress in backward areas. This helps alleviate Chinas uneven development.

 Finally, we conduct a sensitivity analysis of our model and analyze the strengths and weaknesses of our model.

 Keywords: Factor Analysis, Comprehensive Evaluation, CIPP

Introduction

Problem Background

 Nowadays, with the rapid development of science and technology, the social demand for specialized talents is rapidly increased.At the same time, the rise of the overall cultural level of citizens is also imminent, which is conducive to the development of the country.Higher education is the further professional and vocational education for students on the basis of the completion of secondary education.We can try to meet both of these needs through this activity.

 Up to now, there are 233 countries and regions in the world with different education systems, each with its own advantages and disadvantages.It is very important to have a healthy and sustainable state for a country or region’s higher education.Therefore, it is necessary to build a model to measure and evaluate whether a higher education system is healthy and sustainable.This model will enable the country to better formulate relevant policies to make improvements, so as to achieve its own continuous development.

Our work

  1. Firstly, we establish an evaluation model of the health status about higher education which is applicable to all countries and apply it to several countries for analysis and select a country where there is room for improvement in its higher education system.

  2. Then we develop an attainable and reasonable vision for the selected country.we propose target policies and implementation timelines to support migration from the current state to the proposed state.

  3. Furthermore,we use our model to measure current and improved systems in selected countries.And evaluate the effectiveness of our proposed policies.

  4. Last but not least, we discuss the impact of the policy on the real world during the transition and final stages of the implementation phase.

Service Quality Evaluation

 Higher education is a service provided by society and the state for citizens.So,a healthy higher education system must meet the needs of citizens. Therefore,the service quality with national education can be used as an important indicator of the health of the higher education system.
 The concept of “service quality perceived by consumer” was put forward in 1982 by Professor Christian Gronroos,a well-known Finnish marketing scientist. And he believes that service quality is a subjective category that exists in the minds of consumers. So the best way to assess service quality is to survey citizens’ satisfaction. And in order to make higher education services better meet the needs of customers, we also need to investigate the clients assessment of the status quo.

Gap Analysis Model

 The famous American management scientist Zeithaml and others proposed “Gap Theory” as early as 1985. We used this theory to build a gap analysis model.
 In order to simplify the expression, in the following, we refer to consumers of higher education or service objects of higher education and higher education service personnel as customers and service personnel respectively.

 We use the gap between the customer’s true perception of service quality and the customer’s expectation of service quality to measure service quality.When investigating customer opinions on the service, we refer to QS ranking data. From the picture above we can see that this gap mainly comes from three aspects:

  • Whether the service staff understand the customer’s needs or not
  • Whether the service personnel comply with the regulations or not
  • Other social background factors

 All these factors will form the result.When these factors have a negative impact,this will result in the quality of service actually received by the users is lower than the quality of service expected by the users. As a result, customer satisfaction with the service will decrease. When this happens, certain evaluation scores of the national higher education system will drop.

TASK 2 : Improvement Strategy

 In view of the current problems in China’s higher education system, we decided to improve in three aspects.
1. Economic input
 Increase total education expenditure from 4% to at least 5%. The proportion of higher education investment remains at the current 32%. This can be improved appropriately, but it should be controlled within 40%, so as not to reduce the education funding of primary and secondary schools.
2. Educational process

  • Increase the enrollment rate of higher education by increasing the number of enrollment and increasing the number of schools, making it increase from the current 40% to 50% or higher.
  • Increase the number of masters and doctors by expanding the scale of graduate enrollment. Doing so can increase the proportion of master’s and doctoral degrees to 8% and 1% and above respectively.
  • Pay more attention to improving students’ practical ability in talent education.

3. Teaching system
 Through the reform of the teaching system, we will find a more suitable teaching method for China, so as to enhance the strength of institutions and make China and Chinese institutions higher in the world rankings. Effect Evaluation

 In order to better understand the effects of our policies and make improvements in the future, we have conducted a more in-depth analysis of their effects. In this process, we established the CIPP model.

 The CIPP evaluation model is a decision-centered education evaluation model proposed by the American educator Stufflebeam in 1967. As for education evaluation, American educators describe it as “a systematic survey of educational goals and their strengths, weaknesses and values”. Now, the cipp evaluation model is widely used in the field of evaluating educational effects. It can provide information for our educational decisions.

Construction of CIPP Model

 The CIPP evaluation model is mainly divided into four parts: context, input,process and product.

  1. Context Evaluation
  • Fund guarantee
    A healthy education system should meet the funding required for basic daily teaching tasks. And having an overall development plan and clear financial and incentive policy support is more conducive to the progress of higher education.
  • Higher education development positioning
    The development direction of higher education should adapt to practical teaching and promote the improvement of students practical ability. The talent training model of the higher education system established by the government, universities and society should be advanced and scientific.
  1. Input Evaluation
  • Teacher ability
    Whether the university has enough teachers with relevant professional qualifications and whether the management team can meet the normal teaching management needs are all factors we must consider.
  • Facilities and equipment
    Colleges and universities should have a certain building area and independent teaching places. With the advancement of science and technology, the demand for computer network equipment in teaching continues to increase. Therefore, we should constantly update the system to meet this demand.
  • Curriculum structure
    During the teaching process, fully discussed and verified course materials should be provided for students to learn. There should be clear requirements for student learning and result evaluation system.
  1. Process Evaluation
  • Management system
    In order to improve efficiency, universities must have complete regulations and a dedicated team of experts responsible for evaluation.
  • Teaching quality monitoring
    Through the establishment of a college evaluation system, evaluations are carried out from the four aspects of colleges’ basic situation, development prospects, management capabilities, and competition talent delivery results to help understand colleges’ operating conditions.
  1. Product Evaluation
  • Student employment and social evaluation
    The actual construction achievements of colleges and universities are mainly reflected in the three aspects of the first employment rate, whether the employment direction and the study major are compatible, and social satisfaction. To strike a balance between developing students scientific knowledge and practical skills is the primary task of construction.
  • Featured Project
    Schools should rely on their own advantages to establish innovative projects in their own fields.

But we need to know that to receive higher education, we must first accumulate certain knowledge.So the increase in the number of students receiving primary and secondary education will help increase the number of students receiving higher education.
 Because our policy is not only aimed at higher education, but also involves all levels of education, it has positive significance for both primary and secondary education. This produces the result shown in the figure.

Impact on Society and the Country

 1. In today’s rapid development of science and technology, the expansion of high-level talents is the cornerstone of national development. Our policy is closely aligned with the times and satisfies this demand well. The direct effect of it is to increase the number of people receiving higher education. It has great benefits in terms of economy and technology. This accelerates the future development of the country.

 2. . Our policy has stimulated progress in backward areas. This helps alleviate Chinas uneven development. Some underdeveloped regions are not without development potential, but due to geographical reasons, they have not received attention. Our policies have changed this situation and provided strong support to these areas so that they can fully exploit their potential.

 3. As our policy strengthens the supervision of teaching management personnel, on the basis of increasing education investment, more education investment can be better used in the teaching process. At the same time, it helps to establish discipline and prevent some corruption.

 4. Our policy advocates the principle of combining learning knowledge with practice, and encourages scientific research, development and innovation. This creates a good learning atmosphere. The whole people explore science and development together, and society and the country will get better and better.

Sensitivity Analysis

 In the talent quality evaluation model, we calculate the value of 𝑛 by dividing the proportion of the number of people with a bachelor’s degree or above in a country by the proportion of the number of people with a bachelor’s degree or above in the world. For the convenience of expression, here we use 𝑀 to represent the proportion of bachelor degree and above in the world. In fact, M is not a constant value, so we let 𝑀 change, and then observe the changes in the results to explore the sensitivity of the model.

 As we all know that $Q=AK^n$, $n=\frac{\LARGE{x_1}}{M}$. And the functional relationship between F and Q can be abbreviated as $F=aQ+b$. So, we can get the formula that $Q=AK^{\frac{\LARGE {x_1}}{M}}$.

 We find the first derivative of $Q$:

$ Q'=\frac{dQ}{dM} $= $ A\frac{x_1^2}{M^3}K^{\frac{x_1}{M}-1} $

 And $F$ and $M$ have a similar relationship,so we can consider that:
$$F=a AK^{\frac{x_1}{M}}+b\ \ \ \ \ \ F’=\frac{dF}{dM}=\frac{dF}{dQ}\frac{dQ}{dM}=a\frac{dQ}{dM}=aQ’$$

 As shown in the this figure, when 𝑀 changes,𝑄 and 𝐹 do not change much. Whenever 𝑀 increases by 0.05, 𝑄 and 𝐹 will not change more than 10% . This has the same meaning as shown in the figure below.

 And the values of 𝑄′ and 𝐹′ are very close to 0, which indicates that the change of 𝑀 has little effect on 𝑄 and 𝐹. At the same time, considering that the proportion of the number of people with bachelor and above degrees in the world is unlikely to change dramatically in a short period of time, we finally believe that our model is stable.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • The data on which we build the model and the predictions come from official websites and official journals, which are more rigorous.
  • Our model fully takes into account the national power gaps between different countries, and balances the impact of the economic differences between developing countries and developing countries on the evaluation of education by introducing a satisfaction model, so it has strong applicability and universality.
  • Our model can track the health of higher education in a country for a long time, and evaluate the health of higher education at any point in time.
  • When the actual situation changes, the parameter index of the factors of the model can be adjusted to adapt to the new environment, which is flexible.

Weakness

  • • In order to simplify the model and facilitate calculations, we assume that religion, race, gender, etc. have no influence, but the higher education of the actual country is often affected by these factors.
  • When establishing the talent quality evaluation model, we believe that all of the individuals knowledge and abilities come from the national education system,but in reality the level of knowledge of the human group will also come from the family society.
